About Me

I have been in the court reporting/captioning business for over 25 years. I began as an official court reporter with Los Angeles Superior Court and was one of the early adopters of a realtime courtroom, writing realtime in every trial for both the judge and the clerk. I later had the honor of writing for the first hearing-impaired juror to serve in the State of California and later went on to craft the first NCRA guidelines for CART in the courtroom. I then took on the challenge of live captioning for VITAC, covering news, shopping, and sports. Since leaving VITAC, I have been a freelance captioner/CART provider. My jobs have covered the gamut from highly technical web captioning for Microsoft, IBM, and other large corporations, to one-on-one CART for students in the classroom, to onsite captioning at large conventions and even the inaugural ball. With the event of COVID, I have shifted to captioning directly into Zoom.


If there is an accommodation you need in any form, I am certain I can provide it. Whether accommodating one person or a group of 100 or more, I have the equipment and the setup you need and will work together with you to provide the look and feel you want in your captions.
